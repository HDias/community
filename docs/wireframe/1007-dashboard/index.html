Summary & Recommendation

Five directions for the community Dashboard, evaluated against the core goal: an actionable overview that surfaces what needs the admin's attention now and attaches an action to each item.

OptionPhilosophyBest forActionabilityBuild risk
1 · Card Grid Stat cards over a recent-activity panel — the existing pattern. Fastest ship; familiar to any admin. ★★☆☆☆ Low
2 · Action Feed ★ Ranked "attention required" list; each item has context + a primary action. Admins who want a to-do list, not a report. ★★★★★ Med
3 · KPI Board Metric tiles with trend deltas + B&W charts + defaulter mini-table. Data-minded admins tracking direction over time. ★★★☆☆ Med
4 · Widget Columns Configurable masonry of per-domain widgets with "view all" links. Multi-role teams wanting a tailorable overview. ★★★☆☆ High
5 · Command Center Dismissible alerts strip + quick-actions bar over an activity timeline. High-tempo admins who act and clear all day. ★★★★☆ Med

★ Recommended: Option 2 — Action Feed

The Action Feed most directly serves the stated goal. It ranks what needs attention (defaulters, pending bylaw acks, upcoming meeting, term ending) and attaches a single primary action to each row, so the dashboard becomes a worklist rather than a wall of numbers — while raw totals stay available in a thin header strip. Option 5 (Command Center) is the strong runner-up and shares much of the same DNA; Option 1 (Card Grid) is the safe fallback if the priority is shipping the existing pattern quickly. Options 2 and 5 can even be phased: launch the feed, then add the quick-actions bar.
